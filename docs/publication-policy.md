# Publication Policy

## Public Voice

The public tone should be personal but professional:

- sincere and plain;
- technically precise;
- bilingual when useful;
- clear about uncertainty;
- free of trading hype;
- not written like a strategy sales page.

## What The Repository Should Prove

It should show that the author can:

- understand market structure;
- turn intuition into testable hypotheses;
- build baselines;
- account for costs and execution;
- read failure modes honestly;
- organize research into a reusable knowledge system.

## What It Should Not Claim

It should not claim:

- guaranteed profitability;
- live-trading readiness unless separately validated;
- universal parameter optimality;
- that archives are hidden successes;
- that a backtest is investment advice.

## Recommended Public Disclaimer

```text
This repository is for research and education only. It is not investment advice, trading advice, solicitation, or a promise of future performance. Backtests are sensitive to data, execution, cost, liquidity, and regime assumptions.
```
