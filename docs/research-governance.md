# Research Governance

The research process follows a simple order:

```text
market fit
-> gross edge
-> strategy hypothesis and baseline
-> net edge after friction
-> promote, narrow, or archive
```

## Principles

1. Market fit comes before strategy elegance.
2. Gross edge is tested before detailed friction work.
3. A hypothesis must exist before code.
4. The first test should be a simple baseline.
5. New modules are observations first, not trading rules.
6. Complexity must pay for itself through robustness, lower tail risk, better execution, or better portfolio utility.
7. Falsification is a valid output.
8. Old conclusions can be superseded, but should not be silently deleted.

## Promotion Labels

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Active baseline | Strong enough to remain a current research baseline |
| Candidate | Worth tracking, but not final |
| Stabilizer | Useful at portfolio level, not necessarily high standalone alpha |
| Observation | Interesting evidence, not a strategy |
| Archived | Tested and not promoted |
| Superseded | Replaced by newer evidence |

## Archive Standard

Every archived line should preserve:

- original hypothesis;
- tested path;
- key result;
- reason for not promoting;
- what the result still teaches;
- future condition that could reopen it.
